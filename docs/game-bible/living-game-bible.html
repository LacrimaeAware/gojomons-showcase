The Game In One Breath

Pick a Master, catch and train a team, move through nine type-themed towns, and survive a chain of routes, gyms, shops, events, and boss checks. The battles play themselves. The decisions before the battle are where the player wins.

Build The Team

Draft creatures across a run, manage a party and PC, replace moves, buy items, take relics, and decide when a risky capture is worth the slot.

Read The Route

Each town offers routes, services, rest, shops, and encounters. The best run is not the one that fights everything. It is the one that knows what it can afford.

Let It Resolve

Combat is automated through shared rules. Move choice, type matchups, styles, relics, items, and party order decide whether the team acts smart when pressure hits.

Live

Playable spine

Main menu, master selection, starter trail, town menu, walkable route, battle, capture, rewards, PC, shop, gym, next-town choice, final boss, and end run.

Lab

Decision model

The faster-moving campaign model tests town services, resolve, route previews, elite rewards, campfires, and wider 2v2 pressure before those pieces land in the live path.

Needs Your Call

  • Blacksmith shape: tiers, upgrade cost, target objects, Machine discount/perk, and whether quality multiplies numbers or unlocks authored effects.
  • Final-boss prep: guaranteed rest/prep day before the last boss, or keep the current immediate pressure.
  • Terminal-death spread: accept boss-heavy skilled losses as the cost of soft-loss recovery, or push more unrecoverable risk into mid-run.
  • Machine starter identity: which placeholder mons become real early anchors, and whether converter-mons are a deliberate Machine pattern.
  • Evolution content: which families deserve higher-BST evolved forms or branched item evolutions first.

Needs Structured Test

  • Capture access: scarce resource, targeted shop/event access, free auto-roll, party cap, and PC pressure should be tested as one policy instead of scattered tweaks.
  • Route preview timing: how much level/type/reward/boss info the player sees before committing.
  • Reward tiers: every elite/boss should not default to a relic; rewards need basic, prestige, gear, stat, move, and special-route lanes.
  • Limited battle input: switching, Master ultimate, or form trigger can stay an experiment, but full manual move control remains outside the current identity.
  • Orbit map scope: keep the day-menu spine unless the map improves real routing decisions quickly.

The Run

A run starts with a Master and a starter, then moves through a repeating town week. The rhythm is simple enough to read and wide enough for build decisions.

Choose from rolled Masters
Take starter and opening kit
Clear starter trail
Survive nine town weeks
Face the final boss

Opening Trail

The starter trail is deliberately forgiving: first capture, opening boon, rare catch, then the first town. It gets the player into the run with a small team instead of a fragile solo start.

Three Worlds

The long-form structure is three worlds of three towns. Each world clusters types, threats, and services so the route from first town to finale feels authored without becoming a free-roam map.

Day 1

Route, shop, rest, event, PC, or mirror fight. Start reading the week.

Day 2

Another action day. Routes are usually the main XP, gold, and capture engine.

Day 3

Forced town day in the current planner. Upkeep and services matter.

Day 4

Last normal prep day. The lab can hide a major prize fight here.

Day 5

Gym day. Win for a relic reward and the next region choice.

Routes

Routes are short seeded room chains with fights, caches, heals, shops, events, elites, and finales. The route is not just a corridor. It is a risk contract.

Calm Route

Safer level curve, ordinary payout, and a better fit when the team needs reliable growth before the gym.

Perilous Route

Higher enemy level and stronger rewards. In the lab, this is where the weekly major elite fight can appear.

Route previews make the decision readable: biome, level, room count, finale, reward boost, common types, and whether the team is walking into a matchup problem.

Combat

No attack buttons. The player expresses skill through roster, lineup, held items, relics, master choice, route choice, capture choices, and move replacement. Then the fight resolves.

Smart Move Choice

The move picker weighs damage, same-type bonuses, strong matchups, accuracy, status logic, shield logic, target types, and each creature's rolled style.

Styles Matter

Sweepers chase decisive hits, supports favor sustain and utility, disruptors like statuses and multi-hit pressure, tanks favor reliability, and balanced creatures split the difference.
